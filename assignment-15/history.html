PHONEIX MERCURY

HISTORY

OCTOBER 30th, 1996

The WNBA announces the eight teams to complete in its inaugural season in 1997 and Phoenix is awarded a franchise.

The team is officially named the Mercury on February 14, 1997.

JANUARY 27th, 1997

The WNBA announces the eight teams to complete in its first season in 1997 and Phoenix is awarded a franchise.

The team is officially named The Mercury on February 14, 1997.

APRIL 17th, 2004

With the first pick in the WNBA Draft, Phoenix selects Diana Taurasi from the University of Connecticut.

She went on to win the WNBA Rookie of the Year Award the following season.

SEPTEMBER 16th, 2007

The Phoenix Mercury beat the Detroit Shock in the finals to win their first ever WNBA championship.

Phoenix was down 2 games to 1 at one point during the finals, but ended up winning with a score of 108-92.

APRIL 15th, 2013

With the first pick in the WNBA draft, the Phoneix Mercury select Brittney Griner from Baylor University

After beign drafted, she becomes the first player in WNBA history to record 2 dunks in the same game.

SEPTEMBER 12th, 2014

Once again, the Phoenix Mercury sweep Chicago to become WNBA Champions.

Diana Taurasi earns WNBA Finals MVP after averaging 20.3 points each game.

JUNE 18th, 2017

Diana Taurasi passes Tina Thomson on the WNBA all time leading scoerers list after making her 7,489th shot.

FEBRUARY 12th, 2020

The Phoenix Mercury trade and sign 4x-WNBA All Star, Skylar Diggens-Smith, having it be one of the most shocking trades in WNBA history.

Diggins-Smith went on to team with Diana Taurasi as the only two players to rank in the top 10 in both scoring and assists during the 2020 season.

SEPTEMBER 15TH, 2020

In round 1 of the playoffs, Shey Peddy of the Phoneix Mercry hits the game winning shot against the Washinton Mystics; the team the cut her ealier in the season.

Phoenix won 85-84 and advance to Round 2 in the playoffs

JULY 15, 2021

The Phoneix Mercury form global partnership for newly named Footprint Center to become a transformative venue or futures to come.

The Phoenix Mercury will share this arena with the mens team; The Phoneix Suns of the NBA.

JAN 24th, 2021

The Phoneix Mercury sign new head coach Vannessa Nygaard for the 2022-2023 season.

In the 1998 WNBA draft, she was selected by the New York Liberty in the fourth round (39th overall pick).
